## Definition

**Library Method™** is a structured, human-aware methodology for capturing, classifying, verifying, and retrieving work history, decisions, and operational knowledge as defensible, auditable evidence.

It is designed for professionals, teams, and organisations that require provable records — especially in environments involving governance, compliance, regulated AI systems, and legal accountability.

The methodology's name derives from the epistemic architecture of library science: knowledge is only useful if it is captured, classified, verified, and retrievable. Provenance is not a feature. It is the foundation. What cannot be traced cannot be trusted. What cannot be trusted cannot scale.

---

## The Problem Library Method™ Solves

Most systems generate activity, not truth.

In professional and organisational contexts, records are typically:
- fragmented across tools and platforms
- captured for one purpose and pressed into service for another
- undocumented in provenance — no record of who decided what, when, on what basis
- reconstructed after the fact rather than structured at intake

In the age of AI, this problem compounds. AI systems amplify whatever they are fed. Flawed inputs do not produce flawed outputs in isolation — they produce flawed outputs at scale, with confidence, across thousands of decisions simultaneously. The error has no natural ceiling.

Library Method™ intervenes before that moment — at the intake layer, before structure is lost and before provenance becomes irrecoverable.

## The Four Stages

Library Method™ operates as a continuous cycle across four stages:

### 01 — Capture

Collect events, decisions, deliverables, and outputs in natural workflow — without destroying the signal.

The principle: **no garbage in.** Capture is engineered so that error does not become data, and data does not become doctrine. The integrity of what is collected determines the integrity of everything built on it.

### 02 — Classify

Apply a stable taxonomy: categories, priority, context, and identifiers that survive time, tool changes, and organisational drift.

Classification is not overhead. It is the precondition for retrieval, comparison, governance, and meaningful AI assistance. Structure imposed at intake is recoverable. Structure that must be retrofitted is not.

### 03 — Verify

Make records tamper-evident through integrity checks, provenance tracking, and — where required — cryptographic anchors.

Verification turns work history into defensible evidence. Your record should be provable, not reconstructed. The difference between a claim and evidence is a verifiable chain of custody.

### 04 — Retrieve

Surface trusted history instantly — for governance review, performance accountability, litigation readiness, regulatory compliance, and clarity under pressure.

Retrieval is the purpose the preceding three stages exist to serve. A record that cannot be retrieved is not a record. A record that can be retrieved but not trusted is worse than no record at all.

## Core Principles

Library Method™ rests on four operating principles:

1. **No garbage in.** Clean inputs create clean outputs. The quality of what enters the system determines the quality of everything the system can produce.

2. **Structure first.** Classification is the precondition for every downstream use — governance, analysis, legal accountability, and AI assistance. Structure imposed after the fact is always a reconstruction. Reconstruction is never as reliable as original capture.

3. **Truth as an asset.** A verifiable record is a productive asset. An unverifiable claim is a liability. The methodology treats provable work history as something with genuine operational and legal value.

4. **User ownership.** People have the paramount right to govern their own records — access, correction, portability, and evidentiary integrity. This principle aligns with GDPR, the EU AI Act, and evolving accountability standards for automated decision systems.

## Origin

Library Method™ was not designed as a product and then applied to practice. It was named in 2025 after being operated as a personal information architecture for decades.

The underlying discipline — structuring inputs at intake, preserving provenance, designing for retrievability rather than volume — emerged from direct observation of the same structural failure mode across four domains over more than forty years:

- **Aviation (1989–1997):** America West Airlines. At an airline, input failures surface in real time, with an aircraft that needs to push back in six minutes. The system does not compensate for incorrect inputs. It executes them faithfully, at speed.
- **Financial markets (1997–1999):** Wells Fargo Bank, N.A., Foreign Exchange Sales & Trading. The Deal Book — *Customer Has / Customer Wants* — was a human validation layer designed before the trade was entered. The bank's position is always derivable. The entry does not permit ambiguity.
- **Consumer technology at scale (1999–2009):** Apple Inc., Senior Product Analyst, AppleCare. AntennaGate resolved in three days instead of the standard six weeks — not a speed story, a data quality story. Structured inputs, properly designed, made the system answerable. Back-purposed data would have produced noise at scale.
- **European governance (Paris):** A different cultural contract. Opacity requires justification. The institutional responsibility for automated decision-making systems, later formalised in GDPR and the EU AI Act, was already the operating standard.

The question that persisted across all four domains was the same:

> *How do you know that what went in was fit for purpose?*

Library Method™ is the answer that survived contact with all of them.
